Why is that demagogues rise to power so frequently through democratic means?
David Bateman in his recent book and during this discussion provides a potential answer by examining how this occurs in several different Western societies over extended periods. Well-positioned people ('elites') often work towards furthering their advantages through means and alliances. This often involves two potentially unsavory actions --"othering" of some who are comparatively disadvantaged, and at the same time ignoring potential flaws in those who are more advantaged--as long as these actions lead to further their relative positions.
