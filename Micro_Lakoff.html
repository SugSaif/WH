George Lakoff

George Lakoff is among the leading public intellectuals of our time, having written several books intended for lay audiences in addition to his considerable academic output. One way to understand his philosophic approach is to compare it to the more traditional view of thinkers such as Steven Pinker. Lakoff and Pinker have had a many decade long debate on the nature of mind, which became somewhat acrimonious and public around the time Lakoff published his book 'Whose Freedom' (in which he sought to answer what conservatives mean by freedom, how conservatives achieve a 'reversal' of freedoms as they are commonly understood, and why they do it*), which Pinker largely panned in the New Republic referring to it as a 'put-up job'. Lakoff gave a scathing response to this critique, accusing Pinker of misunderstanding his work.

While this public display didn't put either of them in their best light, the discourse highlighted some fundamental issues and controversies in the fields of cognitive science, philosophy of mind and linguistics, and their potential implications for our political discourse.
